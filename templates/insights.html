Key Insights & Findings

  • 💡 Students with higher GPA tend to report healthier eating habits, suggesting a link between nutrition and academic performance.
  • 🥗 Seniors consume slightly fewer fruits and vegetables on average compared to freshmen, highlighting a potential decline in diet quality over time.
  • 💰 Higher-income students show increased eating out frequency, which may correlate with higher calorie intake and more processed foods.
  • 🏋️ Exercise frequency tends to drop after sophomore year, indicating the need for better promotion of physical activity among upperclassmen.
  • 🌍 Comfort food choices and ethnic cuisine preferences vary widely by cultural background, suggesting that targeted meal options on campus could improve dietary satisfaction and health.

These insights provide actionable information for designing better meal plans, health programs, and nutrition education initiatives on college campuses.
